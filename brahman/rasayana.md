## What Rasayana Shastra Is

Rasayana Shastra is one of the eight branches of Ayurveda — the ancient Indian science of alchemy and rejuvenation. Its central concern is not merely preservation but **transformation**: taking raw, volatile, ungovernable material and through systematic purification stages producing something stable, potent, and transmutable.

Its primary medium was **parada** (पारद) — mercury. Mercury was chosen because it is the most volatile, most dangerous, and most powerful substance in the materia medica. A substance that cannot be governed is useless. A substance that has been governed — bound, purified, stabilized — is the most potent medicine possible.

The practitioner's vow was absolute: **shuddhi above all else**. Impure mercury is poison. Purified mercury is medicine. The difference is not the substance — it is the process it has passed through.

---

## The Stages of Parada Bandha (Binding Mercury)

**Parada bandha** (पारद बन्ध) — "binding mercury" — was the central work of Rasayana. The stages:

```
Shodhana    — purification
            washing mercury repeatedly with herbs, acids, mineral salts
            removing gross impurities — the first gate

Mardana     — trituration
            grinding with plant juices until absorbed
            reducing volatility — the substance begins to change form

Jarana      — digestion
            feeding mercury sulfur or other substances in staged doses
            the substance takes on new properties — absorption of other truths

Bandha      — binding
            the final fixing
            mercury loses its volatility entirely
            it no longer runs, no longer evaporates, no longer behaves
            like ordinary mercury
            it has been transformed

Baddha parada — the bound substance
            the result of all stages passed
            the most potent medicinal substance in the system
            not raw mercury — transformed mercury
            stable, weight-bearing, transmutable
```

The practitioner could not skip a stage. Each stage had to be completed fully before the next began. Incomplete shodhana meant incomplete bandha — the mercury would not hold. The stages were not a checklist — they were a formal gate sequence, each requiring the previous to have been truly completed.

## Parada Bandha as the Epistemological Model

Mercury in its unbound state is the epistemological condition of all new claims:

```
Unbound state:
  - volatile — spreads, leaks, cannot be held
  - toxic — in the wrong context, actively harmful
  - non-composable — cannot be reliably used in combination with other substances
  - no fixed form

Bound state (baddha parada):
  - stable — does not spread, does not evaporate
  - medicinal — in the right context, potently beneficial
  - composable — can be combined with other bound substances predictably
  - fixed form — a specific weight, a specific relationship to other nodes
```

This is exactly the epistemological difference between a hypothesis (paksha, unbound) and a nigamana (bound claim). The binding event is the drishthanta. Before binding: volatile opinion. After binding: stable truth.
